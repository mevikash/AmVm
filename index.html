Hi! I'm Vikash Meghwal — a Dual Degree (BS-MS) graduate in Chemical Science from the Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur (2024). I hail from Ajmer, Rajasthan, and I'm passionate about interdisciplinary science at the frontier of chemistry and physics.

Currently, I'm working as a Senior Research Associate at the Molecular Functional Materials Research Group, IIT Kanpur, where my project focuses on "Enhancing the Electronic-Coupling and Band Gap Tunability in Ferrocenyl Molecular Ultra-Thin Film by Pd & Cu Doping."

My research intersects Physical Chemistry, Semiconductor Physics, and Materials Science. I'm skilled in advanced spectroscopic techniques (AFM, STM, XPS, NMR), computational modeling, and data analysis for probing material properties at the atomic level.

Beyond research, I have a keen passion for web development, data science, Flutter/Dart, and building things that make science more accessible.

Research Projects

Current

Ferrocenyl Molecular Ultra-Thin Films — Electronic Coupling & Band Gap Tunability

Investigating Pd & Cu doping in monolayer ferrocenyl films (FcC3, FcC5, TCPBA) on Au(111) surfaces. Using STM, AFM, and XPS to characterize electronic structure and band-gap properties at the nanoscale.

Mentor: Prof. T. G. Gopakumar — Molecular Functional Materials Research Group, IIT Kanpur

Poster RSC Paper
SURGE 2023-24

Structure & Dynamics of Synergistic Mixed Solvent Systems

Elucidating molecular interactions in mixed solvent systems using NMR spectroscopy, vacuum distillation, and advanced data analysis. Focused on hydrophobicity's role in synergistic solvation.

Mentor: Prof. Pratik Sen — Biophysical Chemistry Research Group, IIT Kanpur

Poster Report Certificate
UG Research

SARS-CoV-2 Membrane Fusion Mechanism

Studied SARS-CoV-2 fusion via an opportunistic membrane model, analyzing receptor binding and spike-protein priming mechanisms. Explored bio-nanoparticle interactions relevant to viral entry pathways.

Supervisor: Dr. Nagma Praveen — Chemical Biology Lab, IIT Kanpur

Role of Hydrophobicity in Synergistic Solvation

Investigated how hydrophobicity drives synergistic behavior in mixed organic solvents. Techniques included purification of organic solvents, NMR spectroscopy, and vacuum distillation.
